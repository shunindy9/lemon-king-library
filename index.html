◆ THE SOUND
I create music using physical analog gear
for artists, filmmakers, and creators
who need original, textured, emotional sound.
CASSETTE TAPE
Type I, II & IV cassettes. C-60 through C-120. AKAI GX-635D + Sony TC-K700. The hiss is not a mistake — it is part of the texture.
OPEN REEL RECORDER
TEAC A-3440. ¼" tape at 7.5 and 15 IPS. Tape loops, speed manipulation, physical splice editing. The sound of magnetic iron oxide.
VINYL RECORDS
Technics 1200 + Shure V15. Direct-to-tape cuts. Vinyl as an instrument — not just playback. Crackle, groove noise, warmth.
ANALOG SYNTHS & HARDWARE
Roland Juno-106. Moog Grandmother. Boss RE-201 Space Echo. No plugins in the signal chain — only voltage, magnetism, and air.
Who this is for
If you are a filmmaker who needs a score that sounds lived-in and real — not synthetic.
If you are an artist releasing music on physical media.
If you need sound design that cannot be generated by AI.

This is that sound.
◆ WHY THIS SOUND

ANALOG
CANNOT
BE FAKED

I started recording to cassette because I could not afford digital equipment.

Then I realized — the limitations were the sound.

Tape saturation. Speed instability. Oxide dropout. Signal bleed.
These are not problems to fix. They are the texture that makes music feel real.
